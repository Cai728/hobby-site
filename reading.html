Reading

Firstly, let me give you some context from what I mean by "reading". I am a History graduate, studying history, or just an humanties subject in general requires a lot of reading of academic literature, even though acadamic reading can be somewhat boring. I enjoyed and still enjoy reading historic books (the non acadamic kind, you must be neurotic to enjoy reading academic literature as an pastime). My favourite books are on Ancient Rome. Below are a few I've read.

SPQR: A History of Ancient Rome by Mary Beard:

Mary Beard's "SPQR" offers a captivating journey through the history of ancient Rome, providing a meticulous and accessible exploration of the city's evolution from its mythical beginnings to the height of the Roman Empire. Beard combines scholarly depth with a storytelling flair, bringing to life the politics, culture, and daily life of ancient Romans.

Rubicon: The Last Years of the Roman Republic by Tom Holland:

Tom Holland's "Rubicon" delves into the dramatic and tumultuous final years of the Roman Republic, chronicling the rise of figures like Julius Caesar and the events that led to the Republic's collapse. With a gripping narrative and a keen eye for detail, Holland paints a vivid picture of the political intrigues, military campaigns, and larger-than-life personalities that shaped this pivotal period in Roman history.

How Rome Fell: Death of a Superpower by Adrian Goldsworthy:

Adrian Goldsworthy's "How Rome Fell: Death of a Superpower" delves into the various causes behind Rome's decline and fall, offering a balanced assessment of military, economic, and political factors. Goldsworthy, known for his expertise in Roman history, provides a readable and insightful account of the challenges that eroded Rome's dominance and eventual collapse.
